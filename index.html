Business Intelligence System from Scratch

Programming is critical for making smart business decisions based on real-life data. In this process, data collection, storage, processing and visualization are the core modules making up a Business Intelligence (BI) system. With the passion to use programming to solve real-life problems, I built a BI system from scratch.

Move the mouse cursor above each module to see more detailed introduction. For demos of our data visualization module, check stock clustering. For demos of our machine learning algorithms, check strategy detection.


The framework of BI system sheds a light upon a complex application delivery chain via which Applications are delivered. It gives rise to Application Performance Management (APM), the translation of IT metrics into business meaning that that focuses on monitoring and managing the performance of applications.

Notebly, today's APM is facing the challenge that the delivery chain has becoming more and more mobile and distributed, reliant on third parties, cloud-based, complex and fragile. Among those trends is one of the hottest debated topic of cloud-computing. With software-as-a-service (SAAS) the most popular type, cloud-computing brings a lot of advantages and has become more and more popular.

Mine the Connections among Stocks

It has been widely accepted that Domino Effect of Board Linkage exist in security market. Intuitively thinking, prices of certain stocks tend to go up and down together, either because they are in the same industry or because they are from the same region. This is especially meaningful for portfolio management.

Here we provide an interactive demo to demonstrate how such data mining techniques help improve human's understanding of the stock market. Given the stocks (specified by symbols) in the textbox below, we are able to identify their structure from the price correlations in 2012, and visualize such structure with a graph.

We've already put an example set in the textbox. Click "Run!" to build the visualization. You can also modify the symbols (should be indexed by S&P 500) by yourself and check the results. If the default stocks are used, it's obvious that the financial stocks and IT stocks are separated into two clusters within our visualization.

This demo involves python for stock data crawling, javascript for data analysis and visualization.

World Demographic Analysis

In 2006, Hans Rosling gave a talk "the Best Stats You've Ever Seen" in which he shared his finding with regards to global health trend. Here we reproduced this animated dashboard showing how pupulations of countries across the world have been developing from 1963 to 2013. Specifically, we are interested to see overall trends in fertility, life expectacy and population.


The workbook can be downloaded here

We draw fertility rate as horizontal axis, overall life expectancy as vertical axis, and the size of red cirle denoting the size of population. It could be inferred that over the course of around five decades, globally fertility rate has been decreasing while life expenctancy increasing, meawhile total popultaion has been increasing as well. In particular, Asia population and average life expenctancy saw much significant increase compared with other regions, indicating a massive expansion of this region.
